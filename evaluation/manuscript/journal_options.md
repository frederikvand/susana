# Journal Options for SOM Dune Development Manuscript

**Working title:** "Optimizing Alternative Sediments for Dune Construction: An Eco-Geomorphological Approach"

---

## Tier 1 — Strongest Fit (Recommended)

### Ecological Engineering (Elsevier)
- **IF:** ~4.0 | **Scope:** Design and management of sustainable ecosystems
- **Why this fits:** The journal is explicitly oriented toward nature-based solutions,
  ecosystem engineering, and the beneficial reuse of waste materials. Your "eco-geomorphological
  approach" using dredging by-products in a dune-in-front-of-dike (DfD) system maps directly
  onto the journal's mission.
- **Framing angle:** Emphasise sustainable sediment reuse, SOM as a soil amendment for dune
  establishment, and the design implications for coastal protection.
- **Template:** `template/elsevier/` (elsarticle.cls, Harvard referencing)
- **Status:** Template available locally. Good first target.

### Coastal Engineering (Elsevier)
- **IF:** ~5.5 | **Scope:** Coastal processes, offshore structures, beach/dune management
- **Why this fits:** Your XBeach storm erosion evaluations and coastal resilience framing are
  squarely within scope. Multiple foundational references you cite (Mendez & Losada, Roelvink
  et al.) are from this journal.
- **Framing angle:** Lead with storm-season erosion outcomes under alternative sediment scenarios;
  position SOM and aeolian transport dynamics as secondary mechanisms.
- **Template:** `template/elsevier/` (same Elsevier template applies)

## Tier 2 — Strong Fit

### Geomorphology (Elsevier)
- **IF:** ~3.7 | **Scope:** Processes, forms, and evolution of land surfaces
- **Why this fits:** Long-term geomorphological development of dunes, biomorphogenic feedbacks,
  and aeolian-vegetation coupling are core Geomorphology topics.
- **Framing angle:** Centre the manuscript on morphodynamic evolution; position SOM as a
  soil-physical driver of landscape-scale dune formation.
- **Template:** `template/elsevier/` (same Elsevier template applies)

### Earth Surface Processes and Landforms (ESPL, Wiley)
- **IF:** ~3.8 | **Scope:** Coupled physical and biological surface processes
- **Why this fits:** Your quantification of how SOM alters threshold shear velocity and aeolian
  sediment supply rates matches ESPL's preference for process-quantification studies.
- **Framing angle:** Emphasise the dose-response relationship between SOM concentration and
  aeolian transport thresholds; Living Dunes simulations as the geomorphological outcome.
- **Template:** Wiley — use `template/elsevier/` structure but switch to Wiley format on acceptance.
  NOTE: Wiley MEE template already available in living_dunes_projects/projects/release_paper/

## Tier 3 — Broad Audience / Applied

### Science of The Total Environment (STOTEN, Elsevier)
- **IF:** ~9.8 | **Scope:** Multi-disciplinary environmental science
- **Why this fits:** Multi-method studies (greenhouse + field + multi-year simulation) are a
  STOTEN strength. Beneficial reuse of dredging by-products aligns with their sustainability
  framing.
- **Framing angle:** Position as a sustainability assessment: can alternative sediments substitute
  for primary sources in coastal protection NbS?
- **Template:** `template/elsevier/` (same Elsevier template applies)

### Journal of Applied Ecology (British Ecological Society)
- **IF:** ~5.5 | **Scope:** Applied ecological science, management implications
- **Why this fits:** Dose-response curves for marram grass performance based on SOM; ecology-first
  framing around vegetation establishment on alternative substrates.
- **Framing angle:** Lead with vegetation performance and the ecological mechanisms; physical
  outcomes (aeolian transport, storm resilience) as consequences.
- **Template:** BES format — separate template needed on final submission.

## Tier 4 — Specialised

### Aeolian Research (Elsevier)
- **IF:** ~2.5 | **Scope:** All aspects of aeolian science
- **Why this fits:** If the manuscript is trimmed to focus on dynamic organic cohesion and
  pedotransfer-function-based aeolian transport thresholds, this is the top niche outlet.
- **Framing angle:** Methodological contribution — Saxton & Rawls pedotransfer function for
  aeolian threshold estimation from SOM content.
- **Template:** `template/elsevier/` (same Elsevier template applies)
